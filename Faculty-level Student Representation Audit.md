# Faculty-level Student Representation Audit
This document outlines a process by which student leaders within a particular faculty can conduct an audit for how students are represented within programs, departments, and the entire faculty.

**Who?** While any student can conduct this audit, the individuals to whom this work is most relevant would be student leaders within the faculty (e.g., leaders of an academic club for a program in the faculty; student representatives within one of the departments)
**What?** The audit consists of determining the specific clubs and student representation roles within each department, program, and faculty.
**Why?** The benefits of this audit are to compare the different practices for student representation and potentially identify improvements.
**How?** See the steps below for conducting the audit.

**Steps:**
1.	Select a student leader who is committed to conducting the audit.
2.	Reach out to the faculty to explain the audit
a.	Explain why and how they are conducting an audit
b.	Take feedback about the process 
c.	Ask about how students may be represented at the faculty level (e.g., faculty council, faculty club)
d.	Ask for support in connecting with individual departments and programs to complete the audit
3.	Connect with individual program and departments to fill in the remaining cells of the table.
